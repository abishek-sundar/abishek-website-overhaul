My Projects

2018 Orbis Challenge

This challenge consisted of developing an AI to play a game, in which four snakes fought each other in a 1v1v1v1 style. The game was focused on territory acquisition and trying to keep our snake alive.

We secured 7th place in head-to-head competition against over 200 participants and 70 other submissions.

Voyager - The Ultimate Touristic Map

This is a mapping software that gives you point-to-point directions, auto-completes your search queries, has a mini-map for easy navigation and much, much more.

This project taught me the basics of using version control (SVN) and comprehensive unit-testing. We wrote this program entirely in C++.

Duck Hunt

We coded the classic game, Duck Hunt, using NIOS-II Assembly on an FPGA board. We made our very own light gun that works on LCD screens from 2-3 feet away after some calibration for the environment it is being used in.

This project taught me how interrupts work and to use GPIO as an interface. It required us to use all our knowledge in electronics and low-level programming, which made it extremely satisfying to work on.

Brick Breaker

We made the game, Brick Breaker, in Verilog-DESL on an FPGA board as well. The objective of the game was to destroy all the bricks on the screen without missing the ball. We added a "fast ball" mode as well which made the game tougher to play. We implemented pause functionality and allowed the user to play the game using a PS/2 keyboard.

I learned that one clock cycle error can show itself in the program minutes after the actual bug took place. This really hit timing, systematic debugging and testing home.

F1 design engineer

I took part in F1 in schools in two different years and had podium car finishes both years. I was involved with coming up with multiple designs, creating worthy prototypes, testing for lift, drag, weight etc. and choosing the best to eventually hit the track after simulation first, then real-world testing. I rendered the cars in different environments and helped create VR and 360 animations as well to present the car during the competition.

This entire process was unlike anything I had done previously and had a huge learning curve, which I cherish to this day.
